About

Quaesitor was a one of kind event that took two stages to complete. The event was an innovative take on the general concept of idea pitching contests and rapid fire quiz competitions. We combined the two effectively, to create a platform that was enjoyable as well as academically useful for participants from all fields of engineering.

All resources regarding this event was hosted on our website as well as our IEEE Student Branch's instagram handle, @ieeersetsb.

Guidelines

  1. The competition shall start on 23rd April, 2020 at 9:00 a.m.
  2. The event will be conducted in 2 sections.
  3. The first section comprises of 4 problem statements and consecutive idea pitching sessions. This section will be held over four days, with one problem statement per day.
  4. The second section consists of a rapid-fire quiz competition
  5. The problem statements will be posted in the Instagram page of the IEEE student branch of Rajagiri School of Engineering and technology and this website at 12 p.m everyday. The Instagram handle is @ieeersetsb.https://instagram.com/ieeersetsb?igshid=16nrg0k9ft3hh
  6. Details for the rapid-fire session will be released when the 1st section is over.
  7. The time for submission of idea abstract will be 12 p.m noon everyday. Each problem statement carries 15 marks and the criteria of evaluation will be based on practicality and effectiveness of the provided solution.
  8. The participants must submit the solution to the problem statement within 24 hours or else marks will be deducted correspondingly. For a delay of each 10 minutes 1 mark will be deducted. Submission will be done via google forms circulated along with the email or through the website.
  9. Solution should be made into a document (Microsoft word file or PDF) and submission will be done via Google forms circulated along with the email or in the website. The word limit for the abstract is a minimum of 100 words and the file size should be less than 10 mb.
  10. Any sort of plagairism will not be tolerated.
  11. There will be one problem statement per day for four days. On the 5th and final day (27th April, 2020) of the competition, a rapid fire session will be conducted which will be based on the problem statements given on the last 4 days.
  12. The final results will be based on the overall score gained by the participants.

Rapid-Fire Quiz Guidelines

  1. The participants may attend the rapid-fire quiz anytime from 2:00 p.m. to 10:00 p.m. on 27th April, 2020.
  2. The allotted time is 3 minutes, within which they must submit the form. After three minutes of opening the form, you will lose 2 points for every 10 seconds you are late to submit it.
  3. The total number of questions is 30. Participants need not attend all questions. Try to submit in time more that completing all the questions.
  4. Please note that the score shown immediately after the quiz by the google form itself does not take your time consumption into consideration. The final score after taking into consideration the time taken for submitting the quiz will be published on the next day by 3 p.m on 28th of April 2020. The top three from the leaderboard will be decalred the winners.
  5. The participants are allowed to attend the session one time only.
